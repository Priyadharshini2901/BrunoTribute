Diana's Charitable Work

Diana was renowned for her extensive charitable work. She devoted herself to numerous causes, using her platform to make a positive impact on the world.

One of her most notable contributions was her involvement in raising awareness about AIDS. In the late 1980s, when misconceptions about the disease were widespread, Diana played a crucial role in changing public perception and breaking down the stigma associated with AIDS.

Additionally, she championed campaigns against landmines, advocating for their global ban. Her efforts contributed to the signing of the Ottawa Treaty in 1997, which aimed to eliminate the use of anti-personnel landmines worldwide.
